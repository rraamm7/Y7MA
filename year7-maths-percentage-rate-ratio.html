📖 Percentage

Per cent means "per hundred." A percentage is a fraction with a denominator of 100. We use the symbol %. Percentages, decimals and fractions all represent the same value in different forms.

🔑 Key Formula: Percentage of a quantity = (Percentage ÷ 100) × Quantity
1

Converting between forms

To convert a percentage to a decimal, divide by 100. To convert a decimal to a percentage, multiply by 100.

45% = 45÷100 = 0.45
0.72 = 0.72×100 = 72%
3/4 = 0.75 = 75%
2

Finding a percentage of a quantity

Divide the percentage by 100, then multiply by the total quantity.

30% of 200 = (30÷100) × 200 = 0.3 × 200 = 60
15% of 80 = 0.15 × 80 = 12
3

Expressing one quantity as a percentage of another

Divide the part by the whole, then multiply by 100.

18 out of 24 = (18÷24) × 100 = 0.75 × 100 = 75%
4

Percentage increase & decrease

Find the change, divide by the original, multiply by 100.

Price: $50 → $40. Decrease = $10
% decrease = (10÷50)×100 = 20%
Sale price after 20% off $50 = 50×0.8 = $40

📖 Rate

A rate compares two quantities measured in different units. Rates are written with a slash — e.g. km/h, $/kg, L/min. A unit rate is the rate for one unit of the second quantity.

🔑 Key Idea: To find a unit rate, divide the first quantity by the second quantity.
1

What is a rate?

A rate compares two different units. Unlike ratio, rates always have units attached.

80 km/h → kilometres per hour
$3.50/kg → dollars per kilogram
15 L/min → litres per minute
2

Finding the unit rate

Divide the first quantity by the second to find the rate per 1 unit.

300 km in 3 hours → 300÷3 = 100 km/h
$4.50 for 6 apples → $4.50÷6 = $0.75 per apple
3

Best buy — comparing rates

Find the unit rate (cost per gram or per item) for each option. The lower unit rate is better value.

400g for $3.20 → $3.20÷400 = $0.008/g
600g for $4.50 → $4.50÷600 = $0.0075/g
∴ 600g is better value
4

Speed, distance and time

Speed = Distance ÷ Time. Rearrange to find any missing value.

Speed = D÷T   Distance = S×T   Time = D÷S
Example: 240km at 80km/h → Time = 240÷80 = 3 hours

📖 Ratio

A ratio compares two or more quantities of the same kind. Ratios are written with a colon — e.g. 3:5. Unlike rates, ratios have no units. We simplify ratios just like fractions.

🔑 Key Idea: To simplify a ratio, divide both parts by their Highest Common Factor (HCF).
1

Writing and reading ratios

A ratio a:b means "for every a of one thing, there are b of another." Order matters!

8 red : 12 blue → simplified: 8÷4 : 12÷4 = 2:3
"2 red for every 3 blue"
2

Simplifying ratios

Find the HCF of both numbers, then divide both by it. Keep going until no common factor remains.

15:25 → HCF=5 → 15÷5 : 25÷5 = 3:5
12:18 → HCF=6 → 12÷6 : 18÷6 = 2:3
3

Equivalent ratios

Multiply or divide both parts by the same number to find equivalent ratios.

2:3 = 4:6 = 6:9 = 10:15 (all equivalent)
Check: 4÷6 = 2/3 ✓   6÷9 = 2/3 ✓
4

Dividing a quantity in a given ratio

Add the parts of the ratio to find the total parts. Divide the quantity by total parts to find one part, then multiply.

Divide $56 in ratio 3:4
Total parts = 3+4 = 7   One part = $56÷7 = $8
3 parts = $24   4 parts = $32
