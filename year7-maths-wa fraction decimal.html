📖 Addition & Subtraction of Integers

In Year 7 we extend addition and subtraction to negative numbers. The number line is your best tool — moving right means adding, moving left means subtracting.

🔑 Key Rule: Adding a negative = subtracting. Subtracting a negative = adding (two negatives make a positive!).
1

Adding a positive to a negative

Start at the negative number and move right by the positive amount.

−3 + 7 → start at −3, move 7 right → 4
2

Adding two negatives

Both negative? Add the numbers and keep the minus sign.

−6 + (−4) = −(6+4) = −10
3

Subtracting a negative (double negative)

Two negatives cancel — subtracting a negative is the same as adding.

5 − (−3) = 5 + 3 = 8
4

Result is negative

Subtracting a larger number from a smaller one gives a negative result.

12 − 15 = −(15−12) = −3

📖 Multiplication & Division of Integers

Multiplying and dividing integers follows simple sign rules. Master these and any calculation becomes straightforward!

🔑 Sign Rules: Same signs → Positive result. Different signs → Negative result.
1

Multiplying — same signs

Positive × Positive = Positive. Negative × Negative = Positive.

(−4)×(−3) = +12    5×6 = 30
2

Multiplying — different signs

Positive × Negative = Negative. Always.

(−4)×3 = −12    7×(−2) = −14
3

Dividing integers

Same sign rules apply. Divide the numbers, then apply the sign.

(−15)÷(−3) = 5 (same→+)    (−12)÷4 = −3 (diff→−)
4

Order of Operations — BODMAS

Brackets → Orders → Division/Multiplication → Addition/Subtraction.

3+4×2 → multiply first: 4×2=8 → then add: 3+8 = 11

📖 Fractions & Decimals

Fractions and decimals are two ways to represent the same value. Year 7 focuses on operations with fractions and converting between forms.

🔑 Converting: Fraction → Decimal: divide numerator ÷ denominator. Decimal → Fraction: use place value then simplify.
1

Adding fractions — common denominator

Find the LCD, convert both fractions, then add numerators.

1/2 + 1/4 → LCD=4 → 2/4 + 1/4 = 3/4
2

Subtracting fractions

Same process — find LCD, convert, then subtract numerators.

3/4 − 1/3 → LCD=12 → 9/12 − 4/12 = 5/12
3

Multiplying fractions

Multiply numerators together, multiply denominators together, then simplify.

2/3 × 3/4 = (2×3)/(3×4) = 6/12 = 1/2
4

Converting & comparing

Convert fractions to decimals to compare. The larger decimal is the larger fraction.

3/5=0.6   5/8=0.625 → 5/8 > 3/5
